Life Timeline

  • 1890: Born in a 4 room house in Henryville, Indiana
  • 1895: Father came home with a fever and died that day. Harland's mother found work at a tomato cannery, and Harland was left in charge of caring for his siblings. He became skilled at cooking.
  • 1902: His mother remarried, and the family moved to Greenwood, Indiana
  • 1903: Sanders didn't get along with his stepfather, and ran away at the age of 13. He took odd jobs to get by.
  • 1906: Sanders falisifies his date of bith and enlists in the US Army, serving as a teamster in Cuba.
  • 1907 - 1909: Sanders move around the south, and has a variety of jobs, from blacksmith helper to fireman.
  • 1909: Marries Josephine King. They eventually have three children, a son, Harland Jr., and two daughters, Margaret and Mildred.
  • 1909 - 1915: Sanders studies law by correspondence through the La Salle Extension University. Eventually he begins practicing in Little Rock, Arkansas. His legal career ended after a courtroom brawl with his own client.
  • 1920: Sanders established a succesfful ferry boat company.
  • 1920 - 1930: Sanders takes the money from his ferry boat company and sinks it into a failed venture in acetylene lamps.
  • 1924: Moves to Winchester, Kentucky to work at as a tire salesman. Loses job, and then is asked to run a gas station, which closes in 1930, as a result of the Great Depression.
  • 1930:Shell Oil Company offered Sanders a service station in North Corbin, Kentucky, rent free, in return for a percentage of the sales. Sanders began selling chicken dishes at his gas station, first serving customers in his living quarters, then opening a restaurant. Sanders was involved with a shootout with a local competitor over the repainting of a sign directing traffic to his station.   • 1935: Sanders was commissioned as a Kentucky Colonel by the governor Ruby Laffoon.
  • 1980: Sanders was diagnosed with acute leukemia in July. Died at the age of 90 in December.
